Sample front number three — football — and a story that is true every single summer.

First: nothing moves in the transfer market until everything moves. Clubs are chains of dependencies — the striker your team wants won't be sold until his club signs a replacement, who won't be released until a third club closes its own deal. That's why July is rumors and the last week of August is chaos: it's not drama, it's a dependency graph resolving.

Second: the fee you read in the headline is rarely the fee. Add-ons, sell-on percentages, and payment schedules mean two clubs can announce the same transfer with numbers thirty percent apart and both be telling the truth. When a deal collapses over "personal terms", the interesting question is which of the invisible numbers moved.

Third: watch minutes played, not goals, when judging a summer signing. Goals arrive on a lag; a new forward getting ninety minutes every week is a coach voting with the only currency that matters.

A real football front would track your leagues and your club, with match previews on the mornings that matter. What to watch: the dependency graph, starting to move.
